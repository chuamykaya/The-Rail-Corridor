The Rail Corridor

The Rail Corridor, once paved with tracks that served the Keretapi Tanah Melayu (KMT) railway, is ideal for leisurely trekking or cycling.

Stretching 24km north to south of Singapore, the former railway lines are now replaced with trekking paths.

From Bukit Timah to Tanjong Pagar, traversing the Rail Corridor will bring you through the natural and urban landscapes of Singapore.

The trekking paths are mostly straight and flat, making it an easy hike for most;many families can be seen taking a stroll through the Rail Corridor.

Additionally, there are multiple points where one may access and exit the Rail Corridor, in case you decide not to trek the full 24km path.
